## Auto-Renewal Options Explained

### Option 1: Auto-Renewal With Interest

**What happens:**
- Maturity: Principal + interest = new deposit amount
- Continues earning interest
- Automatic compounding
- More wealth accumulation

**Example:**
- TD1 (91 days): BDT 1,00,000 → BDT 1,02,316
- Auto-renewed as TD2: BDT 1,02,316 earns interest again
- TD2 (91 days): BDT 1,02,316 → BDT 1,04,693
- Compounding benefit: ~2.3% in 6 months

---

### Option 2: Auto-Renewal Without Interest (Principal Only)

**What happens:**
- Maturity: Interest credited to account (you receive)
- Principal renewed as new deposit
- Same amount cycles
- Income stream created

**Example:**
- TD1 (91 days): BDT 1,00,000 matures
- Interest BDT 2,316 credited (yours to keep)
- Principal BDT 1,00,000 renewed as TD2
- Quarterly income: ~BDT 2,316 every 91 days

**Use Case:** Regular income stream while capital preserved!

## FAQs

**Q: What's the difference between 91 and 181 days?**
A: 91-day @ 9.25% for 3-month access. 181-day @ 9.50% for 6-month with slightly higher rate.

**Q: Can I change tenor mid-tenure?**
A: No. Each TD fixed at opening. Open separate deposits for different tenors.

**Q: Is auto-renewal automatic?**
A: Only if selected at opening. Otherwise, funds credited without renewal.

**Q: What happens on maturity?**
A: If auto-renewal selected: Automatic renewal. Otherwise: Profit + principal credited to account.

**Q: Can I close before maturity?**
A: Yes, but interest penalty applies (reduced to Savings rate).

**Q: Can I open multiple TD Plus?**
A: Yes! Open as many as you want with staggered maturities.

**Q: Is interest guaranteed?**
A: Yes. Rate fixed at opening for your tenor.

**Q: Can minors open?**
A: Yes, with parent/guardian operating mandate.

**Q: Is interest taxable?**
A: Yes. Income tax per applicable laws (~10% typical).

**Q: Can I borrow against this?**
A: Yes. Up to 90% of deposit available anytime.
