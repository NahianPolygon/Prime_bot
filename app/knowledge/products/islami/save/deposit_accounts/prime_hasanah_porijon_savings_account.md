# Prime Hasanah Porijon Savings Account

**Tagline:** Supporting Families of Remitters

Prime Hasanah Porijon is a savings account designed for the families of Non-Resident Bangladeshi (NRB) remitters living in Bangladesh. It offers attractive provisional profit, incentives, and complimentary life insurance, helping Bangladeshi remitters living abroad support their family's financial well-being.

---

## Overview

Prime Hasanah Porijon Savings Account is a specialized Islamic account for NRB remittance-receiving families. With 3% provisional profit, tiered life insurance coverage based on remittance amounts (BDT 25K-100K coverage), automatic insurance enrollment upon remittance crediting, local and dual-currency debit card, and family-focused features, this account celebrates remittance relationships.

---

Perfect for NRB beneficiary families receiving remittances from abroad.

## Insurance Details

### Automatic Insurance on Remittance

**How it works:**
1. NRB sends remittance via Prime Exchange
2. Amount credited to account
3. Automatic insurance enrollment (next 30 days)
4. Coverage amount based on remittance tier

**Coverage tiers:**

| Remittance Amount | Insurance Coverage | Validity |
|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Up to 50,000 | 25,000 | 30 days from credit |
| 50,001-100,000 | 75,000 | 30 days from credit |
| More than 100,000 | 100,000 | 30 days from credit |

### Maximum Coverage

- **Highest single coverage:** BDT 1,00,000 per remittance
- **Multiple remittances:** Each gets separate coverage
- **Example:** BDT 120K + BDT 80K in same month = BDT 100K + BDT 75K coverage

## How to Apply?

### Required Documents

1. **Account Opening Form**
   - Completed personal account opening form
   - Beneficiary information

2. **Identity Proof**
   - NID, Passport, or Birth Certificate
   - Applicant and nominee

3. **Address Proof**
   - Recent utility bill
   - Residential address
   - Local address verification

4. **Income/Profession Proof**
   - Salaried: Salary slip
   - Self-employed: Professional cert
   - Remittance verification: Bank statement showing NRB remittances
   - Beneficiary status confirmation

5. **NRB Documentation** (if applicable)
   - Remitter's passport copy (visa/work permit)
   - Relationship proof (marriage certificate, birth certificate)
   - NRB employment letter (if available)
   - Previous remittance statements

6. **Other Documents**
   - Self-attested passport photo (2 copies)
   - Nominee photo (1 copy, attested)
   - Tax Return proof/E-TIN (if applicable)

### Opening Process

1. Visit Prime Bank branch
2. Request "Porijon Savings Account" form
3. Explain NRB remittance relationship
4. Complete application form
5. Submit identity and address proofs
6. Provide NRB verification documents
7. Initial deposit: BDT 500 (minimum)
8. Receive account number
9. Activate MyPrime
10. Register for insurance auto-enrollment
11. Receive debit card

## Insurance Claims

### How to Claim Insurance

1. **Upon remitter death:** Inform bank immediately
2. **Documentation needed:**
   - Death certificate of remitter
   - Proof of relationship (marriage cert, birth cert)
   - Bank passbook showing remittances
   - Insurance claim form

3. **Claim process:**
   - Submit to branch
   - Bank forwards to insurance company
   - Insurance processes claim
   - Payment to beneficiary/nominee

4. **Support:**
   - Contact respective branch
   - Call 16218 for assistance
   - Bank guides entire process

## FAQs

**Q: Must the NRB remitter be a family member?**
A: Yes. Relationship must be proven (spouse, child, parent, dependent relative).

**Q: Is the 3% profit guaranteed?**
A: It's provisional profit, subject to change per bank policy and Bangladesh Bank guidelines.

**Q: What if I don't receive remittance for a month?**
A: Account continues. Insurance only active when remittance received (30-day period).

**Q: Can insurance coverage stack if I receive multiple remittances?**
A: Yes. Each remittance gets separate 30-day coverage based on amount.

**Q: Is this Sharia-compliant?**
A: Yes. Profit as per Islamic banking principles. No interest charges.

**Q: Can I convert to regular savings account?**
A: Yes. Can transition if remittance stops or situation changes.

**Q: How do I receive remittance?**
A: NRB sends via Prime Exchange, direct transfer, or partner channels to your account.

**Q: What happens if remitter becomes NR?**
A: Account continues. Insurance applicable only when remittances received.

**Q: Can I open joint account with NRB?**
A: Yes, if NRB is relative. Both names on account, you are sole operator.

**Q: Is family history affecting insurance?**
A: No. Insurance based on remittance amount, not health history.
