## Overview

Prime Hasanah Personal Retail Account is an Islamic savings account designed for micro-entrepreneurs, informal sector workers, and self-employed individuals who operate without formal trade licenses. With zero-balance opening, no intercity/online transaction fees, free internet banking, and no trade license requirement (only local public representative attestation), this account democratizes banking for the informal economy.

## How to Apply?

### Required Documents

1. **Account Opening Form**
   - Completed personal account opening form
   - Business details section

2. **Identity Proof**
   - NID, Passport, or Birth Certificate
   - Applicant and nominee

3. **Address Proof**
   - Recent utility bill (Telephone/Electricity/Water)
   - Current residential address
   - Business location (if different)

4. **Income/Profession Proof** *(No Trade License Required)*
   - **Local Public Representative Attestation:**
     - Local government official (Union/Upazila Chairman/Member)
     - Ward Commissioner certificate
     - Verification of business/profession
   
   - **OR Professional Association Certificate:**
     - Trade union membership
     - Professional association proof
     - Vocational organization certificate

5. **Proof Documents**
   - Self-attested passport photo (2 copies)
   - Nominee photo (1 copy, attested)
   - Tax Return proof/E-TIN (if applicable)
   - FATCA form

### Opening Process

1. Visit Prime Bank branch
2. Request "Prime Hasanah Personal Retail Account" form
3. Explain business/profession
4. Complete account opening form
5. **Obtain attestation** from local public representative or professional association
6. Submit attestation with application
7. Provide identity and address proofs
8. Initial deposit: Any amount (zero-balance option)
9. Receive account number
10. Activate MyPrime
11. Receive debit card and cheque book

## Attestation Process

### Getting Local Public Representative Attestation

**Who can provide:**
1. Union Parishad Chairman
2. Upazila Parishad Member
3. Ward Commissioner
4. Local government office

**What they verify:**
- Identity confirmation
- Business/occupation verification
- Area resident confirmation
- Income source legitimacy

**How to obtain:**
1. Visit local government office
2. Provide business description
3. Officer verifies through local knowledge
4. Certificate issued (typically 1-2 days)
5. Bring to bank for account opening

**Cost:** Usually free or minimal (5-50 BDT)

## FAQs

**Q: I don't have a trade license. Can I still open account?**
A: Yes! This account is specifically designed for those without formal trade licenses. Local representative attestation is sufficient.

**Q: How do I get local representative attestation?**
A: Visit your Union Parishad, Upazila office, or local government representative. They verify and issue certificate (usually free).

**Q: What if I don't have a fixed business address?**
A: Use your residential address. Business location can be your home.

**Q: Is there any profit on deposits?**
A: No direct profit mentioned. Account designed for transaction convenience, not wealth accumulation.

**Q: Can I use cheque book for business?**
A: Yes! Cheque book facility available. Professional payment option for customers.

**Q: Is intercity transaction fee really free?**
A: Yes. "No intercity transaction fee" is key feature. Transfer between cities at no charge.

**Q: What if my business grows?**
A: Can upgrade to other Prime Hasanah accounts (Savings, Current) as business formalizes.

**Q: Is this Sharia-compliant?**
A: Yes. No interest element. Islamic banking principles maintained.

**Q: Can I maintain account if business changes?**
A: Yes. Account persists. Only documentation needed if income source changes significantly.

**Q: What happens if I stop business?**
A: Account can continue as personal savings account. No issues closing or continuing.
