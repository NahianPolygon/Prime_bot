# Prime Atlas FC Account

**Tagline:** Sail the World, Save at Home

With Prime Atlas FC Account, access a foreign currency current account offering unlimited transactions, seamless remittance transfers abroad, and local currency withdrawals in Bangladesh.

---

## Overview

Prime Atlas FC Account is exclusively designed for **Bangladeshi mariners and crew members working on ocean-going vessels** operated by foreign or local shipping companies. It combines a foreign currency account with unlimited transaction capabilities, enabling seafarers to manage earnings globally while maintaining local access for family needs.

## How to Apply?

### Required Documents

#### For Account Holder (Mariner)

1. **Completed Personal Account Opening Form**
   - Available at any Prime Bank branch

2. **Identity Proof Document**
   - NID (National ID), OR
   - Passport, OR
   - Birth Certificate
   - Note: Introducer required if using documents other than NID

3. **Address Proof Document**
   - Recent copy of utility bill
   - Telephone/Electricity/Water bill
   - Proof of residence in Bangladesh

4. **Income/Profession Proof Documents**
   - **Copy of Valid Passport**
     - Must show stamps of international travel
     - Visa stamps from maritime nations
   
   - **Copy of Mariner's Discharge Book** (Most Important)
     - Official maritime document proving seafaring service
     - Shows rank, vessel details, dates of employment
     - Essential credential for mariners
   
   - **Employment Letter/Work Contract**
     - From local agent confirming next joining date, OR
     - From shipping company with current employment contract
     - Confirms ongoing or next maritime assignment

5. **Other Documents**
   - Self-attested recent passport-size photo (2 copies)
   - Proof of Submission of Tax Return (PSR) or E-TIN Certificate (where applicable)

#### For Nominee(s)

1. **Identity Proof Document**
   - NID, OR
   - Passport, OR
   - Birth Certificate

2. **Photograph**
   - 1 photo of nominee(s) attested by account holder

### Opening Process

1. Visit Prime Bank branch with Discharge Book
2. Explain maritime employment and future vessel joining date
3. Submit Account Opening Form and all documents
4. Bank verifies employment and documents
5. Choose currency (USD, GBP, EUR, or JPY)
6. Open linked Prime One Savings Account (BDT)
7. Account activation same day
8. Receive account details and debit card

## Wage Earners' Bond & NFCD Investments

### Wage Earners' Bond
- **Target Audience:** Bangladeshi workers abroad
- **Currency:** BDT (converted from foreign currency)
- **Returns:** Attractive interest rates (typically 6-7%)
- **Tenure:** 2-5 year options
- **Safety:** Government-backed security
- **Tax Benefits:** Tax incentives for wage earners

### NFCD (Non-Resident Foreign Currency Deposit)
- **Account Type:** Term deposit in foreign currency
- **Currency:** USD / GBP / EUR
- **Returns:** Interest on foreign currency deposits
- **Tenure:** Fixed periods (6 months to 5 years)
- **Flexibility:** Callable options available
- **Benefits:** Higher returns than savings

### Investment Strategy for Mariners

**Year 1-2:** Build savings in foreign currency account
**Year 2-3:** Invest in NFCD for returns
**Year 3+:** Consider Wage Earners' Bond for BDT accumulation
**Result:** Long-term wealth building + family support

## Mariner's Discharge Book

### What is It?

The Mariner's Discharge Book (MDB) is an **official maritime credential** that:
- **Proves seafaring service** to employers and banks
- **Shows rank and vessel details**
- **Documents employment history**
- **Confirms maritime qualifications**
- **Required for account opening**

### Important Information in Discharge Book

- Mariner's name and identification
- Vessel name and details
- Rank/position on vessel
- Date of joining and leaving
- Employer/shipping company
- Sea service record
- Competency certification

### Why Banks Require It

- Verifies legitimate maritime employment
- Confirms international experience
- Ensures account holder credibility
- Banking regulation compliance

## FAQs

**Q: Can I have an account if I work on inland vessels?**
A: No. This account is for ocean-going vessels only (international maritime work).

**Q: Which currencies can I choose?**
A: USD, GBP, EUR, or JPY. Choose based on your earnings.

**Q: Can I have multiple currency accounts?**
A: Typically one Atlas account with one currency. Check with bank for multiple accounts.

**Q: Do I need the debit card?**
A: Only for USD account. It's optional but recommended for convenience.

**Q: How do I access my money while at sea?**
A: Online banking from vessel (if internet available) or request bank wire transfer.

**Q: What if I lose my job or stop working at sea?**
A: Account remains active. You can maintain it for savings/investment purposes.

**Q: Can my family access the account?**
A: Not directly. But you can transfer to their Porijon or Savings account anytime.

**Q: How do I invest in Wage Earners' Bond?**
A: Ask your bank relationship manager. Convert USD to BDT, then invest.

**Q: What's the difference between the linked BDT account and regular Savings?**
A: It's Prime One Savings Account specifically linked to your Atlas account for easy currency conversion and local access.

**Q: Can I get a loan against this account?**
A: Yes. Atlas account holders can apply for loans with special mariner rates.

**Q: Is my money safe?**
A: Yes. FDIC insured (BDT portion) up to maximum limit per Bangladesh Bank guidelines.

**Q: How often can I convert foreign currency to BDT?**
A: Unlimited conversions anytime. No conversion limit.
